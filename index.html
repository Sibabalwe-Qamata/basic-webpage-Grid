Historical Places in Cape Town

Castle of Good Hope

  • The first stone was laid on 2 January 1666 and it was completed by April 1679
  • The Castle was never attacked. Battles against the British were fought at Muizenberg in 1795 and Blaauwberg in 1806
  • The Castle of Good Hope was referred to as “Kui keip” (Stone Kraal) by the Khoi and sometimes “Citadel” by Dutch settlers

City Hall

  • It remains an imposing building, helped in no small part by a restoration of its façade in 1979.
  • The workmanship and materials are of high quality, and the organ made from mahogany, teak and pine.
  • The City Hall's carillon was installed as a World War I war memorial, with 22 additional bells being added in 1925 with the visit of the Prince of Wales.
